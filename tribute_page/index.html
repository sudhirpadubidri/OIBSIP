Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam


Here's a time-line of APJ Abdul Kalam's life:


  • 1931 - Born on October 15th in Rameswaram, Madras Presidency (now Tamil Nadu).

  • 1954 - Graduated in Physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.

  • 1960 - Earned a degree in Aeronautical Engineering from Madras Institute of Technology.

  • 1960 - Joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment (ADE) of the Defence Research and Development Organisation (DRDO).

  • 1969 - Transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) and became Project Director of the SLV-III, India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle.

  • 1980 - Successfully launched the Rohini satellite, marking a significant milestone in India's space program.

  • 1981 - Got Padma Bhushan Award.

  • 1982 - 84 - Led the development of the Polar Satellite Launch Vehicle (PSLV), which became India's workhorse launch vehicle.

  • 1990 - Got Padma Vibhushan Award.

  • 1992 - 99 - Served as the Scientific Adviser to the Defence Minister and played a key role in Pokhran-II, India's second nuclear test.

  • 1997 - Got Bharat Ratna Award, India's highest civilian honor.

  • 1999 - 2002 - Served as the Principal Scientific Adviser to the Government of India and Chairman of the Technology Information, Forecasting & Assessment Council (TIFAC).

  • 2002 - 2007 - Became the 11th President of India.

  • 2007 - 2015 - Continued to be a prolific writer and speaker, inspiring millions with his vision for India's future.

  • 2015 - Passed away on July 27th, while delivering a lecture in Shillong, Meghalaya.
